Care

American Water Spaniels are active sporting dogs that love water and outdoor adventures. Their coat needs regular grooming, and their minds need just as much exercise as their bodies.

üßº

General Care

  • Brush the coat several times a week to prevent tangles and remove dead hair.
  • Rinse or bathe after swimming in dirty or salty water to protect the skin and coat.
  • Check and clean ears regularly, especially after swims, to help prevent infections.
  • Trim nails every few weeks and brush teeth several times per week.

Puppy Care

  • Introduce water gently and make early swimming experiences positive and fun.
  • Begin socialisation with people, children, and friendly dogs as early as possible.
  • Use short training sessions to teach basic manners and house training.
  • Provide soft bedding and safe chew toys to support teething and rest.

Adult Care

  • They need at least 1 hour of daily exercise: walks, runs, retrieving, or swimming.
  • Give them jobs such as fetching from water, carrying toys, or practicing field drills.
  • Monitor weight, as active sporting dogs can lose condition or gain weight if activity changes.

Common Health Issues

  • Ear infections if ears stay damp or dirty after swimming.
  • Hip dysplasia or joint issues in some lines.
  • Allergies or skin irritations occasionally.
  • Dental disease if teeth are not cleaned regularly.

Training Tips

The American Water Spaniel is bright, eager, and sometimes a little independent. They enjoy learning and working with their people, especially when training includes retrieving and water games.

üéì

Basic Training

  • Teach sit, down, stay, recall, and loose-leash walking from a young age.
  • Use positive reinforcement with treats, toys, and praise.
  • Practise impulse control, such as waiting at doors and before jumping off docks or boats.
  • Enroll in puppy or basic obedience classes to build skills and focus.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Daily walks plus swimming, retrieving, or field work keep them happiest.
  • Use fetch games, nose work, and puzzle toys to challenge their minds.
  • They often enjoy dog sports like agility, dock diving, or rally obedience.

Common Challenges

  • Can become bored and vocal or destructive without enough activity.
  • May be stubborn at times ‚Äì keep sessions short, fun, and consistent.
  • Strong hunting instincts mean they may chase birds or small animals outdoors.

Safety Notes

  • Supervise around open water; make sure they can exit pools or docks safely.
  • Use life jackets for boat trips, especially for young or tired dogs.
  • Check ears and paws after long days outdoors to catch problems early.

Fun Facts

  • They were developed to retrieve game from boats on the rivers and marshes of the Great Lakes.
  • Their curly coat helps protect them from cold water and rough cover.
  • Many American Water Spaniels still work today as versatile hunting and family dogs.
  • They form close bonds with their families and often pick a favourite person.
  • The American Water Spaniel is the official state dog of Wisconsin.
