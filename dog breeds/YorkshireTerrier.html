Care

Yorkshire Terriers are small but spirited companions who thrive on attention and indoor life with their families. They need gentle handling, regular grooming, and daily activity to stay healthy and happy.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: Use a high-quality diet designed for toy or small breeds, offered in small, frequent meals if needed.

  • Living environment: Yorkies do best as indoor dogs and should be protected from rough play, falls, and extreme temperatures.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily walks and indoor playtime are usually enough, but many Yorkies happily join their people on longer strolls.
  • Coat & skin: Regular baths, brushing, and trimming around the eyes help keep the coat clean and comfortable.
  • Mind: Puzzle toys, trick training, and interactive games keep their sharp little brains busy.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Gentle, regular walks and soft play help maintain muscles and mobility as they age.
  • Comfort: Provide soft, supportive bedding and help them avoid slippery floors or jumping down from high surfaces.

Seasonal Tips

  • Cold weather: Small dogs can get chilled quickly‚Äîuse sweaters or coats outdoors in chilly weather.

Common Health Issues

  • Dental disease: Small breeds are prone to tartar build-up‚Äîregular tooth brushing and dental care are essential.
  • Luxating patellas: Kneecap issues can affect some Yorkies; keep them at a healthy weight and avoid excessive jumping.
  • Tracheal collapse: A soft harness is often preferred over a collar to reduce pressure on the neck.
  • Eye & skin care: Hair around the eyes should be kept tidy to reduce irritation; monitor skin under longer coats.

Training Tips

Yorkshire Terriers are bright and eager to please, but they can also be independent and a little stubborn. Consistent, gentle training with plenty of rewards works best for this tiny terrier.

Fun Facts

  • Yorkshire Terriers were originally bred as ratters in mills and mines in northern England.
  • Despite their glamorous looks today, Yorkies started as tough working dogs in industrial towns.
  • Their long, silky coat is often compared to human hair and can be styled in topknots, clips, or kept short in a ‚Äúpuppy cut.‚Äù
  • Yorkies are popular city dogs because of their small size and ability to live comfortably in apartments.
  • They often form a strong bond with one or two favourite people and love to be included in daily life.
