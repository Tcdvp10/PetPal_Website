Care

Pembroke Welsh Corgis are sturdy, people-oriented herding dogs who thrive as active family companions. They need regular exercise, mental challenges, and consistent grooming to manage their thick double coats, along with careful weight management to protect their long backs and joints.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: Offer a balanced diet for active, medium-sized dogs and monitor portions‚ÄîCorgis gain weight easily, which can strain their backs and joints.
  • Grooming: Brush several times a week to remove loose hair and reduce shedding. Expect heavier ‚Äúcoat blow‚Äù once or twice a year.
  • Climate: Their double coat offers protection in cooler weather, but they can overheat in hot, humid conditions. Provide shade, water, and rest breaks.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Use a quality puppy food and avoid overfeeding to support steady, healthy growth.
  • Activity: Short, controlled play sessions on non-slippery surfaces. Avoid long stair climbs and repeated jumping while bones are developing.
  • Handling: Get them used to brushing, nail trims, ear checks, and teeth cleaning early.
  • Social skills: Positive socialisation with people, children, and other dogs helps shape a confident adult Corgi.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily walks plus playtime and training keep them fit and content. They enjoy having a ‚Äújob,‚Äù such as obedience or agility.
  • Coat & skin: Regular brushing and occasional baths help manage shedding. Check ears, nails, and teeth weekly.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Shorter, more frequent walks may be easier on aging joints and backs.
  • Comfort: Provide supportive bedding and ramps or steps for getting on and off furniture safely.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Exercise during cooler parts of the day, provide plenty of water, and avoid intense activity in the heat.
  • Cold weather: Many Corgis enjoy cooler temperatures, but watch paws on ice or salted sidewalks.
  • Shedding seasons: During heavy shed periods, more frequent brushing and vacuuming will help manage loose hair.

Common Health Issues

  • Back & spine problems: Their long backs can be prone to strain‚Äîkeep them lean and discourage frequent jumping from high furniture.
  • Hip & joint issues: Monitor for stiffness or lameness and consult your vet about joint support if needed.
  • Eye & genetic conditions: Some Corgis may be prone to inherited eye or nerve conditions; regular vet exams are important.
  • Dental care: Regular brushing and professional cleanings help keep teeth and gums healthy.

Training Tips

Pembroke Welsh Corgis are intelligent, eager learners with a playful streak. They respond well to reward-based training and enjoy having tasks to do, but their herding instincts and strong opinions mean clear, consistent boundaries are important.

  • Begin house training early with a predictable schedule and lots of praise for going in the right place.

  • Handle paws, ears, and mouth gently and regularly to prepare for grooming and vet visits.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Avoid repetitive high-impact jumping, especially off furniture, to protect their backs.
  • Use ramps or steps where possible and support them when lifting.
  • Maintain a healthy weight to reduce stress on spine, hips, and elbows.
