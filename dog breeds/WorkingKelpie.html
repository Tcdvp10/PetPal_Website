Care

Working Kelpies are athletic herding dogs bred to work long hours in tough conditions. They need plenty of exercise, mental challenges, and close interaction with their people to stay happy and well-balanced.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: Offer a balanced diet appropriate for a medium, active dog. Working dogs may need higher calories on heavy work days.
  • Grooming: Weekly brushing usually keeps the coat healthy. Expect heavier shedding once or twice a year.
  • Living environment: Kelpies do best in homes that can provide space and daily activity. They are not suited to a sedentary lifestyle.
üê∂

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Feed a high-quality puppy food and keep them lean to support healthy joints and steady growth.
  • Activity: Provide short, frequent play sessions rather than heavy forced exercise while bones are still developing.
  • Handling: Get them used to being touched, brushed, and having their paws and ears handled from a young age.
  • Social skills: Positive experiences with people, dogs, livestock, and different environments help shape a confident, stable adult.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Most adult Kelpies need at least one to two hours of activity daily, including brisk walks, off-leash runs in safe areas, or structured work.
  • Coat & skin: Check regularly for burrs, grass seeds, or minor scrapes, especially if they work in fields or rough terrain.
  • Mind: Training, scent games, and herding-style activities are important to prevent boredom and destructive behaviour.
üßì

Senior Care

  • Movement: Continue daily walks but adjust intensity as they age. Watch for stiffness or reluctance to jump.
  • Comfort: Provide a soft bed and a warm, draft-free resting spot, especially after exercise.
  • Health checks: Regular vet visits help monitor teeth, joints, and weight. Ask your vet about supplements or pain relief if needed.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Work and exercise during cooler hours. Provide shade and plenty of fresh water, especially for dark-coated Kelpies.
  • Cold weather: Their coat offers some protection, but young, elderly, or very lean dogs may still appreciate shelter or a coat.
  • Shedding seasons: During coat blows, brush more frequently to remove loose undercoat.

Common Health Issues

  • Joint concerns: Like many active breeds, some Kelpies may be prone to hip or elbow issues‚Äîmaintain a healthy weight and avoid overdoing high-impact exercise when young.
  • Eye conditions: Certain hereditary eye issues can appear; responsible breeders screen breeding dogs.
  • Dental care: Regular tooth brushing and dental checks help prevent tartar build-up and gum disease.

Training Tips

Working Kelpies are highly intelligent and driven to work. With clear guidance and positive reinforcement, they become responsive, reliable partners both on farms and in active family homes.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start basic cues like sit, down, stay, and recall early using rewards and calm, consistent handling.
  • Teach polite greetings so they don‚Äôt jump up or nip at heels when excited.
  • Expose them to different people, noises, and environments to build confidence and reduce sensitivity.
  • Practise short, focused training sessions‚ÄîKelpies learn quickly but can become bored with repetition.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Provide daily outlets for their herding instincts with fetch, agility-style courses, or herding lessons where appropriate.
  • Use puzzle feeders, scent games, and trick training to challenge their brilliant minds.
  • Many Working Kelpies excel in sports such as agility, obedience, rally, and flyball.
  • Balance physical work with mental tasks and rest to prevent over-arousal and overuse injuries.

Common Training Challenges

  • High drive: Without enough exercise and structure, Kelpies may chase cars, bikes, or children. Teach strong impulse control cues.
  • Sensitivity: They often respond best to fair, gentle handling. Harsh methods can damage trust.
  • Boredom: Under-stimulated dogs may dig, bark, or chew. Keep their schedule rich in activities.
ü©∫

Health & Safety Notes

  • Teach a rock-solid recall and good manners around livestock and roads for safety.
  • Supervise around very young children and teach kids to interact calmly and respectfully.
  • Warm up before intense activity and cool down afterward, especially in working or sport dogs.

Fun Facts

  • Working Kelpies were developed in Australia to move sheep and cattle across vast, hot, and rugged landscapes.
  • They are famous for their ability to work across rough terrain, jumping onto the backs of sheep to cross tightly packed flocks.
  • Despite their intense work ethic, many Kelpies are affectionate companions who love curling up with their favourite people after a busy day.
  • Kelpies are popular in dog sports like agility and obedience thanks to their speed, agility, and eagerness to learn.
  • Many Working Kelpies form a close partnership with one handler and are known for their impressive ability to respond to whistles and distant cues.
