Care

Beagles are sturdy, active dogs that need daily exercise and careful feeding. Their short coat is easy to maintain, but their strong nose and love of food mean owners must watch weight and safety.

üßº

General Care

  • Brush once or twice a week to remove loose hair and keep the coat shiny.
  • Check and clean ears regularly to prevent infections, as their floppy ears can trap moisture.
  • Trim nails every few weeks and brush teeth several times per week.
  • Use quality flea/tick prevention, especially if they spend time in fields or woods.

Feeding & Weight

  • Feed measured meals rather than free-feeding to prevent obesity.
  • Use healthy treats in small amounts, especially during training.
  • Monitor body shape; Beagles gain weight easily and extra pounds stress their joints.

Puppy Care

  • Socialise with people, children, and other dogs so they grow up confident and friendly.
  • Introduce crate training and house training with a consistent schedule.

Common Health Issues

  • Obesity if food intake is not controlled.
  • Ear infections due to floppy ears and outdoor exploring.
  • Hip dysplasia or joint problems in some lines.
  • Allergies or skin irritation in some individuals.

Training Tips

Beagles are smart and eager, but also independent and easily distracted by smells. Training should be fun, positive, and structured to keep their nose and brain busy.

Basic Training

  • Start early with sit, down, stay, recall, and loose-leash walking.
  • Use high-value food rewards and keep sessions short and engaging.
  • Practise recall in fenced areas; Beagles may ignore commands when following a scent.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Daily walks plus extra playtime or sniffy walks are ideal.
  • Nose work, tracking games, and puzzle feeders are perfect for scent hounds.
  • They often enjoy agility, trick training, and interactive games with family.

Safety Notes

  • Always supervise off-leash time; their nose can lead them far away.
  • Use ID tags and microchips in case they wander.
  • Watch for overheating during very hot weather, especially on long walks.

Fun Facts

  • Beagles have an incredible sense of smell and were bred to track small game.
  • They have a distinctive baying voice that hunters could follow over long distances.
  • Beagles are pack dogs and usually enjoy the company of other dogs.
  • Their friendly, gentle nature makes them popular family pets around the world.
  • Many Beagles work in airports as scent-detection dogs, searching luggage for food items.
