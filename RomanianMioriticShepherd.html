Care

Romanian Mioritic Shepherds are large, powerful livestock guardians bred to live and work outdoors. They do best with plenty of space, secure fencing, and a family that understands guardian breeds. Their thick coat and large body need thoughtful grooming, exercise, and health care.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: Provide a high-quality large-breed diet to support joints and slow, steady growth. Keep them lean to reduce stress on hips and elbows.
  • Grooming: Brush several times a week to prevent mats and remove loose undercoat. Check for burrs, debris, and parasites after time in fields or woods.
  • Environment: They prefer a large, securely fenced yard or rural property. Apartment living is not suitable for this breed.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Avoid overfeeding and excessive jumping to protect developing joints. Use a large-breed puppy formula.
  • Activity: Frequent short play sessions on soft ground are better than long runs. No forced running or stair climbing while growing.
  • Handling: Get them used to brushing, paw and ear checks, and general grooming early, so their large size is easier to manage later.
  • Socialisation: Carefully introduce friendly people, animals, and new places so they grow into confident but stable guardians.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Long daily walks, time to patrol a secure yard, and ‚Äújobs‚Äù like watching property help keep them satisfied.
  • Coat & skin: Regular brushing, especially during shedding seasons, keeps the coat healthy. Check ears, nails, and skin weekly.
  • Mind: They enjoy having a purpose‚Äîbasic training, scent games, or calmly supervising livestock or family activities.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Shorter, gentler walks and non-slippery surfaces help aging joints. Watch for stiffness or difficulty getting up.
  • Comfort: Provide supportive bedding and easy access to indoor and outdoor areas. Keep nails trimmed to prevent slips.
  • Health checks: Regular vet visits to monitor joints, heart, weight, and overall mobility are important in their later years.

Seasonal Tips

  • Cold weather: Their double coat is well suited to cold climates. They typically enjoy snow and chilly temperatures.
  • Hot weather: Take extra care in heat‚Äîprovide shade, cool water, and rest during the hottest hours. Avoid heavy exercise in high temperatures.
  • Shedding seasons: Expect heavy shedding in spring and/or autumn. Extra brushing sessions help manage loose fur.

Common Health Issues

  • Hip & elbow dysplasia: Large guardian breeds can be prone to joint issues. Maintain a healthy weight and consider joint-supportive supplements if your vet recommends them.
  • Bloat (GDV): As a deep-chested dog, they may be at risk. Feed measured meals and avoid intense exercise right after eating.
  • Ear & skin care: Check ears regularly, especially if they spend time in damp or dirty areas. Keep coat clean and well-brushed.
  • Dental care: Regular tooth brushing and dental checks help maintain oral health.

Training Tips

Romanian Mioritic Shepherds are intelligent, independent guardians. They think for themselves and take their protective role seriously, so they need early socialisation, consistent rules, and a confident, patient handler.

  • Teach basics like sit, down, stay, and recall using rewards and calm praise. Keep sessions short and positive.
  • Encourage polite greetings so they don‚Äôt jump or push, especially as they grow into very large adults.
  • Practise handling paws, ears, and tail so grooming and vet visits are easier when they are full-sized.

Fun Facts

  • Their name ‚ÄúMioritic‚Äù comes from a Romanian word related to sheep and pastoral life, reflecting their strong shepherding heritage.
  • Despite their imposing size, well-raised Mioritics are gentle and deeply attached to their families, often acting like calm, shaggy ‚Äúshadows.‚Äù
  • Their long, dense coat helps them thrive in harsh mountain weather, from snow and wind to cold, wet conditions.
