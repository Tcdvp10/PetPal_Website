Care

Shetland Sheepdogs are active, brainy herding dogs who thrive on time with their families. They need regular exercise, mental enrichment, and consistent coat care to stay happy and healthy.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: Provide a high-quality diet suited to active, small-to medium herding dogs. Monitor weight carefully‚Äîlong coats can hide extra pounds.
  • Grooming: Brush at least a few times per week to prevent mats and remove loose undercoat, especially around the mane, behind ears, and trousers.
  • Living environment: Shelties love being close to their people. They do well in homes and apartments as long as they get enough exercise and interaction.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Their joints are still developing‚Äîavoid lots of stairs, long runs, or jumping off high furniture.
  • Activity: Use short play sessions, gentle walks, and simple training games rather than long, tiring outings.
  • Handling: Introduce brushing, nail trims, and teeth cleaning early so the full adult coat is easy to manage later.
  • Social skills: Many Shelties are naturally reserved; gentle positive exposure to people, noises, and places builds confidence.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily walks plus play or training sessions are ideal. Many adults enjoy dog sports like agility or obedience.

  • Mind: Mental work is just as important as physical activity. Use training, puzzle toys, and scent games to keep them engaged.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Switch to shorter but still regular walks. Watch for stiffness, reluctance to jump, or lagging behind.

Seasonal Tips
