Common Behaviours to Identify

Be attentive to these common behaviours when training your dog

Round Spin

Dogs often spin before lying down due to instinct. In the wild, spinning helped flatten grass and clear away insects or debris before resting. Even with modern dog beds, this natural behaviour sticks because it helps them feel comfortable and secure.Spinning helps them adjust their body, fluff their bedding, and find the most comfortable resting position ‚Äî similar to how humans adjust pillows or blankets. Round spinning is a natural behaviour rooted in instinct. Most of the time, it‚Äôs completely normal and simply your dog preparing a comfortable, safe resting spot‚Äîjust as their ancestors did.

Licking Face

Anyone who has ever been around a dog has been subject to dog kisses. Whether you love them or not, you are going to be on the receiving end of some slobber. Dogs kiss you for many reasons. For one, it is a way they would communicate in the wild. It is also the way a mother communicates with her young. She licks them to clean them or stimulate them to breathe when they are born. Another reason is one many people already think: the dogs are showing affection. Licking their guardian releases endorphins that can comfort dogs. The next time your dog gives you big kisses, they just want you to know they really like being around you.

Tail wagging is another key communication method. A right-side wag usually means excitement or happiness, while a left-side wag can indicate nervousness or caution. Observing speed, direction, and body language gives clues to how a dog feels.While many people assume a wagging tail always means a dog is happy, tail movements can actually signal a wide range of feelings depending on the speed, height, direction, and looseness of the wag.

Positive Reinforcement

Positive reinforcement in dog training is a method where you reward the dog for the behaviors you want to see more often

  • The dog performs a desired behavior (example: sits when asked).
  • You immediately give a reward (treat, praise, toy, or affection).
  • The dog associates the behavior with something good and is more likely to repeat it.
  • Mix walks with sniff time, fetch, and simple training games.
  • Use puzzle toys and indoor games on rainy or snowy days.
  • Watch for heavy panting, limping, or slowing down ‚Äî those mean it‚Äôs time to rest.

Advanced Training

Advanced dog training refers to higher-level skills and behaviors that go beyond basic obedience like sit, stay, or heel.

    Advanced training usually includes:

  • More complex commands
  • Longer durations
  • Greater distance from the handler
  • More distractions
  • Higher expectations for consistency and precision
