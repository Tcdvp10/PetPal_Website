Bernese Mountain Dog

Good-natured, calm, and strong. They are known for being affectionate, loyal, and eager to please their family. While typically gentle, they retain a certain self-composure and can be aloof with strangers. They are a versatile farm dog, capable of drafting/dragging carts and droving cattle.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Use a large-breed puppy food to support slow, steady bone development and protect joints.
  • Activity: Avoid forced running, jumping, long stairs, or slippery floors while joints are developing.
  • Handling: Start gentle grooming, paw handling, and vet-style checks early so they learn to relax.
  • Learning: Begin basic obedience and socialisation in short, positive sessions.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily moderate exercise (walks, play, light drafting) to keep muscles strong and prevent weight gain.
  • Coat care: Maintain a regular brushing routine to manage shedding and keep the coat clean and comfortable.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Shorter, more frequent walks are easier on aging joints than long, strenuous outings.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Very heat-sensitive. Exercise only in the coolest parts of the day; provide shade, water, and never leave them in a car.
  • Cold weather: Built for snow and chill‚Äîmost Berners love winter, but still need shelter and dry bedding.
  • Rain & mud: Dry thoroughly after wet walks to prevent skin issues and discomfort.

Common Health Issues

  • Cancer: Sadly very common in the breed (e.g., histiocytic sarcoma). Regular vet check-ups are essential.
  • Hip & elbow dysplasia: Can lead to lameness and arthritis; maintain a healthy weight and controlled exercise.
  • Bloat (GDV): A life-threatening emergency in deep-chested dogs. Learn the signs and discuss prevention with your vet.
  • Eyes & blood: Conditions like PRA and Von Willebrand‚Äôs disease can appear; responsible breeders screen for these.

Training Tips

Bernese Mountain Dogs are gentle, eager to please, and sensitive. They do best with calm, positive training and clear routines. Think ‚Äúslow and steady‚Äù rather than high-pressure or high-intensity.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Begin early socialisation with new people, places, sounds, and surfaces.
  • Teach sit, down, stay, and recall using treats, praise, and a soft voice.
  • Keep sessions short (5‚Äì10 minutes) to avoid boredom and joint strain.
  • Practise calm behaviours such as settling on a mat and waiting at doors.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Focus on gentle, steady walks and play rather than intense running or jumping.
  • Use sniffy walks, food puzzles, and low-impact games to exercise their mind.
  • Avoid long stairs, rough play, and hard surfaces while they are still growing.
  • In warm weather, walk during cooler hours and provide plenty of water and shade.

Common Training Challenges

  • Pulling on leash: Use a front-clip harness and reward walking beside you, starting in quiet, low-distraction areas.
  • Shyness or sensitivity: Pair new experiences with treats and let your dog set the pace.
  • Clinginess: Practise short, positive alone-time and reward calm behaviour when you leave and return.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Keep them at a lean, healthy weight to reduce stress on joints and heart.
  • Ask your vet about safe activity levels, especially during puppy growth.
  • Be extra cautious in hot weather‚Äîthey overheat easily with their thick coats.

Fun Facts
