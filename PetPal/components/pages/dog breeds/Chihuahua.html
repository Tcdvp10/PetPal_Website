Care

Chihuahuas are tiny, lively companions who thrive on human attention. They‚Äôre easy to exercise in small spaces but do need careful handling, regular vet care, and appropriate protection from both heat and cold due to their size.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: High-quality small- or toy-breed food, measured portions to prevent obesity. Their small stomachs do best with smaller, more frequent meals.
  • Grooming: Smooth-coat Chihuahuas need weekly brushing; long-coat dogs benefit from several brushing sessions per week to prevent tangles behind the ears and on the tail.
  • Training & socialisation: Intelligent but can be opinionated. Use gentle, reward-based training and introduce them early to new people, sounds, and environments to avoid fearfulness.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Very delicate when young. Watch carefully to make sure they eat regularly and monitor for signs of hypoglycemia (weakness, wobbliness, disorientation).
  • Activity: Short, gentle play on soft, non-slippery surfaces. Avoid stairs, high furniture, and rough play that could cause injury.
  • Handling: Teach calm, gentle handling from the start. Support their body fully when lifting and supervise young children closely.
  • Learning: Begin house training, crate training, and basic cues in short, upbeat sessions with tiny treats.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: A couple of short daily walks plus indoor play usually meet their needs. They enjoy exploring, but their small legs tire faster.
  • Coat care: Continue routine brushing. Check ears, nails, and skin regularly; long coats may need occasional trims around the feet and rear.
  • Mind: Food puzzles, trick training, and short training sessions keep them mentally stimulated and help prevent nuisance barking.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Gentle walks and light play; adjust duration if they seem stiff or tire quickly.
  • Comfort: Provide warm, soft beds and ramps or steps if they‚Äôre used to getting on furniture.
  • Diet: Senior diets or adjusted portions can help maintain a healthy weight. Regular dental care is especially important in older Chihuahuas.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: They can overheat quickly. Walk during cooler parts of the day and provide shade and fresh water.
  • Cold weather: Chihuahuas get cold easily. Many appreciate sweaters or coats for chilly walks and cosy blankets at home.
  • Rain & mud: Short legs mean they get wet and dirty fast; towel-dry after walks and check paws for irritation.

Common Health Issues

  • Dental disease: Toy breeds are prone to tartar buildup and tooth loss. Daily brushing and regular cleanings are recommended.
  • Luxating patella & joint issues: Avoid excessive jumping and keep them at a lean weight.
  • Tracheal collapse: Use a harness instead of a collar for walks and avoid harsh pulling.
  • Heart & eye conditions: Regular vet check-ups help monitor heart murmurs and eye health.

Training Tips

Chihuahuas are bright and eager to bond with their people, but they can also be strong-willed. Consistent, positive training and early socialisation help them grow into confident, polite companions instead of bossy watchdogs.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start socialisation early with calm, positive introductions to people, gentle dogs, and everyday sounds.
  • Teach sit, down, recall, and polite greeting using tiny treats and lots of praise.
  • House training can take time with a small bladder; take them out frequently and reward success immediately.
  • Set boundaries from the beginning so they don‚Äôt learn that growling or barking gets them their way.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Short daily walks and indoor games like fetch or tug are usually enough to meet their exercise needs.
  • Snuffle mats, treat balls, and simple trick training provide excellent mental enrichment.
  • Allow safe off-leash play in secure, fenced areas where larger dogs won‚Äôt overwhelm them.
  • Give them safe, cosy spots to rest, like beds or covered crates where they can feel secure.

  • Possessiveness: Some may guard their favourite person or spot; work on sharing and mat training.
  • Stubborn streak: Keep sessions short and fun, and use high-value rewards to keep them motivated.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Use a harness rather than a collar to protect their neck and trachea.
  • Supervise interactions with children and larger dogs‚Äîthey‚Äôre easily injured if dropped or stepped on.
  • Because they‚Äôre tiny, falls from furniture, rough play, or being underfoot can cause serious injury; manage the environment carefully.

Fun Facts

  • The Chihuahua is considered one of the world‚Äôs smallest dog breeds, often weighing just a few pounds.
  • The breed takes its name from the Mexican state of Chihuahua, where small companion dogs were popular for centuries.
  • Chihuahuas often form a very strong bond with one or two favourite people and love to snuggle under blankets.
  • They come in both smooth and long coat varieties, with many colours and markings‚Äîfrom solid fawn to striking merle patterns.
  • Chihuahuas can live well into their teens with good care; it‚Äôs not unusual for them to reach 15 years or more.
