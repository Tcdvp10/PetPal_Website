English Springer Spaniel

A cheerful, athletic gundog with a constantly wagging tail. English Springer Spaniels are friendly, intelligent, and eager to work or play all day, then happily relax with their family at home. They thrive on activity, training, and being included in everything their people do.

Care

English Springer Spaniels are energetic, affectionate dogs bred to work in the field all day. They need regular exercise, mental stimulation, and consistent grooming to stay healthy, happy, and well-behaved in an active home.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: High-quality diet suitable for medium, active breeds. Adjust portions based on activity level to prevent weight gain.
  • Grooming: Brush several times a week to prevent mats, especially on ears, chest, legs, and tail feathering. Occasional trims help keep the coat tidy.
  • Ears: Long, floppy ears can trap moisture‚Äîcheck and clean regularly to help prevent infections.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Feed a balanced puppy formula. Avoid overfeeding to protect growing joints.
  • Activity: Multiple short play sessions and controlled walks; avoid long runs, jumping, or rough stair use while they‚Äôre still growing.
  • Handling: Introduce brushing, ear checks, nail trims, and gentle baths early so grooming becomes routine.
  • Social skills: Early socialisation with people, dogs, and new places builds a confident adult dog.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Aim for at least 1‚Äì2 hours of daily activity, including brisk walks, play, and off-leash time in safe areas.
  • Coat care: Regular brushing and occasional trimming help manage shedding and keep the coat free of debris from outdoor adventures.
  • Mind: Training games, scent work, and problem-solving toys keep their active brain engaged.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Shorter, gentler walks and low-impact play as joints age.
  • Comfort: Provide soft beds, easy access to favourite spots, and help them avoid slippery floors.
  • Health checks: Regular vet visits to monitor joints, eyes, ears, and heart health are important in older Springers.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Exercise in cooler hours, offer shade and water, and avoid intense midday activity.
  • Cold weather: Their coat offers some protection, but very cold or wet conditions may call for a light dog coat, especially for clipped dogs.
  • Outdoor adventures: After hikes or field work, check for burrs, grass seeds, and ticks, especially in feathered areas.

Common Health Issues

  • Ear infections: Moisture and hair in the ear canal can lead to problems; regular cleaning helps.
  • Hip & elbow dysplasia: Maintain a healthy weight and provide sensible exercise.
  • Eye conditions: Some lines can be prone to eye issues; regular vet eye checks are recommended.
  • Skin & allergies: Monitor for itchiness or hot spots and work with your vet if problems arise.

Training Tips

English Springer Spaniels are intelligent, eager-to-please, and naturally biddable. They respond well to positive, reward-based training and enjoy having jobs to do, whether it‚Äôs learning new tricks, practising recall, or working in dog sports.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start socialisation early with friendly people, dogs, and environments to promote confidence and good manners.
  • Teach sit, down, stay, recall, and loose-leash walking using treats, toys, and praise.
  • Practise calm behaviours like settling on a mat, waiting at doors, and polite greetings instead of jumping up.
  • Keep training fun and varied‚Äîseveral short sessions a day work better than one long one.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Springers love to use their nose. Try scent games, tracking, or ‚Äúfind it‚Äù games in the house or garden.
  • Activities like agility, flyball, rally, or gundog-style retrieval games are great outlets for their energy.
  • Rotate toys and puzzle feeders to keep their mind busy as well as their body.
  • Regular off-leash time in safe areas helps them burn energy and practise recall.

Common Training Challenges

  • Pulling on leash: Their enthusiasm and hunting instinct can make them pull. Practise loose-leash skills and reward walking by your side.
  • Distraction by scents: Build a strong recall and practise in low-distraction areas before moving to more exciting environments.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Because they love water, supervise swimming and rinse their coat afterwards to remove mud or pond residue.
  • Check ears, paws, and feathering for debris and irritation after walks in rough terrain.
  • Use positive training methods‚Äîharsh corrections can damage trust and create anxiety.

Fun Facts

  • English Springer Spaniels were originally bred to ‚Äúspring‚Äù game birds from cover, flushing them into the air for hunters.
  • They are one of the oldest gundog breeds, sharing ancestry with the English Cocker Spaniel before the breeds were separated.
  • Many Springers are known for their ‚Äúsmiling‚Äù expression and constantly wagging tails, earning them a reputation as happy, enthusiastic companions.
  • There are field-bred and show-bred lines; field dogs tend to be lighter and more intense workers, while show dogs often have more coat and a slightly heavier build.
  • Their friendly, trainable nature makes them popular in dog sports, search & rescue work, and as all-around family dogs.
