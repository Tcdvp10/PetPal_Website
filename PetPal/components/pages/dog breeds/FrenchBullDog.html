Care

French Bulldogs are affectionate, people-oriented companions who do best as indoor family dogs. They need moderate daily exercise, careful temperature management, and regular health and skin care, especially because of their flat faces and skin folds.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: Provide a balanced diet appropriate for small, sturdy breeds. Monitor calories to prevent obesity, which can worsen breathing and joint issues.
  • Grooming: Weekly brushing helps remove loose hair. Gently clean facial wrinkles and skin folds to prevent moisture buildup and irritation.
  • Climate: Brachycephalic (short-nosed) dogs like Frenchies can struggle in heat and humidity. Keep them cool and avoid overexertion.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Feed a high-quality puppy formula. Avoid rapid weight gain to support healthy joint and bone development.
  • Activity: Short play sessions on safe, non-slippery surfaces. Avoid long stair climbs or high jumping while they are growing.
  • Handling: Get them used to nail trims, ear checks, and gentle cleaning of facial folds from an early age.
  • Social skills: Early, positive socialisation helps prevent shyness or overprotective behaviour in a small-bodied dog.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily walks and short play sessions usually meet their needs. Focus on quality, not distance, and avoid overheating.
  • Coat & skin: Continue regular brushing and wrinkle cleaning. Check ears and nails weekly.
  • Mind: Training games, puzzle feeders, and gentle play keep them mentally stimulated without overtaxing their breathing.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Shorter, slower walks are usually best. Watch for signs of joint discomfort or breathing changes.
  • Comfort: Provide soft bedding and easy access to favourite spots. Avoid steep stairs or jumping on/off furniture.
  • Health checks: Regular vet visits help monitor breathing, weight, joints, heart, and skin as they age.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Very important for Frenchies‚Äîwalk during cooler hours, provide shade and water, and never leave them in a hot car.
  • Cold weather: Many French Bulldogs appreciate a light sweater or coat in chilly temperatures due to their short coat.
  • Allergies: Some individuals are prone to skin or environmental allergies. Rinsing paws and wiping down the coat after walks can help.

Common Health Issues

  • Breathing difficulties (BOAS): Their flat face can make breathing harder, especially in heat or with exertion. Avoid strenuous exercise and discuss concerns with your vet.
  • Spinal & joint problems: Keep them lean and avoid high-impact activities that might stress the back.
  • Eye & skin issues: Their prominent eyes and skin folds need regular monitoring and gentle care.
  • Dental care: Regular brushing and vet dental checks help keep teeth and gums healthy.

Training Tips

French Bulldogs are smart, people-focused, and often a bit stubborn. With patient, reward-based training and consistent routines, they can learn excellent manners while still keeping their clownish sense of humour.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start house training early with a regular schedule and lots of praise for going in the right spot.
  • Teach sit, down, stay, and recall using small treats and an upbeat tone‚ÄîFrenchies respond well to fun, positive sessions.
  • Practise polite greetings so they don‚Äôt jump up or become too pushy for attention.
  • Introduce gentle handling of paws, ears, and face so vet visits and grooming are easier later on.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Short walks and indoor games like tug, fetch, or gentle training sessions provide good exercise.
  • Puzzle toys, snuffle mats, and ‚Äúfind it‚Äù games stimulate their brain without overworking their body.
  • Play sessions should be broken up with plenty of rest, especially in warm weather.
  • Many Frenchies enjoy basic obedience or trick training as a way to bond with their people.

Common Training Challenges

  • Stubborn streak: Keep sessions short and end on a success. Use high-value rewards and vary the routine to keep them engaged.
  • House training: Small bladders and strong preferences mean accidents can happen. Stay patient and consistent with supervision and schedules.
  • Separation worries: Because they bond closely with people, some may dislike being alone. Practise short, calm departures and arrivals.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Avoid intense exercise, especially in heat, to protect their breathing and prevent overheating.
  • Use a harness rather than a collar for walks to reduce pressure on the neck and airway.
  • Be careful around pools or deep water‚Äîmany French Bulldogs are not strong swimmers due to their build.
