Boerboel

A powerful, confident guardian from South Africa, the Boerboel is a steady, devoted family dog bred to protect home and farm. Calm and watchful with their people yet imposing to strangers, they thrive with experienced owners who can give them structure, training, and plenty of time with the family they love.

Care

Boerboels are large, powerful guardians with big hearts and strong bodies. They are low maintenance in grooming but high maintenance in training, management, and space. Consistent routines, thoughtful socialisation, and responsible ownership are essential.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: High-quality large-breed formula to support joints and steady growth. Measure meals to prevent obesity, which can stress their joints.
  • Grooming: Short coat is easy-care‚Äîweekly brushing, nail trims, ear checks, and regular baths as needed keep them clean and healthy.
  • Training & socialisation: This is not a beginner breed. They need confident, fair leadership, early socialisation, and clear rules from day one.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Boerboel puppies grow rapidly. Use a large-breed puppy food, avoid overfeeding, and keep jumps and stairs limited to protect developing joints.
  • Activity: Several short, gentle play sessions on non-slippery surfaces are better than long, hard exercise.
  • Handling: Get them used to being handled all over‚Äîears, paws, mouth, tail‚Äîso vet visits and grooming are stress-free later.
  • Learning: Start name recognition, recall, and basic manners early with calm, positive reinforcement.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily brisk walks plus secure off-leash time or structured activities (obedience, tracking, nose work) help meet their physical and mental needs.
  • Coat care: Brush weekly to remove loose hair and check skin for irritation, lumps, or parasites.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Shorter, more frequent walks on soft surfaces; adjust activity based on joint comfort and stamina.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Large, dark-coated dogs can overheat quickly. Walk in the cool parts of the day and always offer shade and water.

Common Health Issues

  • Hip & elbow dysplasia: Maintain a lean weight, avoid over- exercise in puppies, and consider joint screening for breeding dogs.
  • Bloat (GDV): Like many large deep-chested breeds, they may be at risk‚Äîask your vet about preventative steps and watch for signs of distress.
  • Heart & eyelid issues: Regular vet exams and screening help catch conditions early.
  • Skin & allergy problems: Some Boerboels may have sensitive skin; work with your vet if you notice itching or recurrent infections.

Training Tips

Boerboels are intelligent, confident, and naturally protective. They learn quickly but need experienced, consistent guidance. Early socialisation, clear boundaries, and positive reinforcement are key to raising a stable adult.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start puppy classes early to build good habits and safe, positive exposure to other dogs and people.
  • Teach sit, down, stay, and recall using food rewards, toys, and calm praise.
  • Practise polite greetings‚Äîno jumping or mouthing‚Äîand reinforce calm behaviour in the home.
  • Decide household rules (couch, door manners, etc.) early and enforce them consistently.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Daily walks, structured play, and training games help burn energy and strengthen your bond.
  • Use scent games, puzzle feeders, and basic tracking or nose-work games to give their brain a workout.
  • A secure, fenced yard is ideal for off-leash play, but they should never roam unsupervised.
  • Rotate toys and make training a part of everyday routines.

Common Training Challenges

  • Over-protectiveness: Poorly socialised Boerboels can become too suspicious. Pair early socialisation with calm, confident leadership.
  • Strength & pulling: Teach loose-leash walking from puppyhood before they reach full size.
  • Stubbornness: Keep sessions short, interesting, and fair‚Äîavoid harsh corrections that can damage trust.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Supervise interactions with children‚Äîteach kids to be respectful and gentle with such a large dog.
  • Because of their size and strength, secure fencing and reliable recall are safety essentials.
  • Schedule regular vet visits, joint checks, and dental care as part of their routine.

  • The name ‚ÄúBoerboel‚Äù comes from Afrikaans: ‚ÄúBoer‚Äù meaning farmer and ‚Äúboel‚Äù loosely meaning big dog or mastiff.
  • Despite their imposing size, well-bred Boerboels are deeply devoted to their families and often form strong bonds with children.
  • They are known for their confident, steady temperament‚Äîcalm at home, but ready to act if they sense a threat.
  • Modern Boerboels can excel in obedience, protection sports, and therapy work when carefully trained and socialised.
