Golden Retriever

Friendly, loyal, and always ready for an adventure with the family.

Size
Medium–Large (55–75 lb / 25–34 kg)
Lifespan
10–12 years
Temperament
Gentle, social, eager to please
Coat
Medium length, double coat, shades of gold

Overview

Golden Retrievers are one of the world’s most popular family dogs. They are known for their kind expression, balanced energy, and love for people of all ages.

Originally bred in Scotland as hunting companions, Goldens are athletic yet easy-going. They thrive in active homes where they can join walks, hikes, or swims, then relax on the couch afterward.

They usually get along well with other dogs and animals, and with proper socialisation they make excellent companions for children. Because they are so people-oriented, they don’t enjoy being left alone for long hours every day.

Best home for a Golden Retriever

  • Families or individuals who enjoy daily walks and playtime.
  • Homes with space to move – a yard is great but not required.
  • Owners willing to brush their coat a few times a week.
  • People who want a soft-natured, affectionate companion.

Training Tips

Golden Retrievers are highly trainable and love to work with their people. Short, fun sessions are the key to keeping their attention.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start socialisation early – new people, places, and sounds.
  • Teach sit, down, stay, and recall using treats and praise.
  • Keep sessions 5–10 minutes and end on a success.
  • Use puzzle feeders to keep their brain busy indoors.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Plan 60–90 minutes of activity per day for adults.
  • Mix walks, fetch, swimming, and sniffy exploration.
  • Avoid long, hard runs until growth plates are closed.
  • Try activities like obedience, agility, or scent work.

Common Training Challenges

  • Mouthing & chewing: provide safe chew toys, redirect gently, and avoid rough play with hands.
  • Pulling on leash: reward walking beside you, practise in low-distraction areas first.
  • Jumping up: ignore jumping and reward four paws on the floor.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Keep Goldens at a healthy weight to protect their joints.
  • Ask your vet before starting intense running or sports.
  • Teach a strong “leave it” to avoid scavenging on walks.

Fun Facts

  • Goldens were developed in Scotland in the late 1800s as hunting and retrieving dogs.
  • Their water-repellent double coat helps them stay warm while swimming in cold lakes.
  • They are one of the most common breeds used as guide dogs, therapy dogs, and search-and-rescue partners.
  • Most Golden Retrievers love carrying toys or objects in their mouths – it’s literally in their job description.
  • Despite their fluff, they are usually very tolerant of grooming and enjoy the extra attention.
