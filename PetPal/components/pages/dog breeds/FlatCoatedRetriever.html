Care

Flat-Coated Retrievers are energetic, affectionate retrievers bred to work on both land and water. They need plenty of daily exercise, mental enrichment, and regular grooming to stay healthy, happy, and well-mannered in an active home.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: High-quality food formulated for active, medium‚Äìlarge breeds. Monitor weight and adjust portions if activity changes.
  • Grooming: Brush the coat several times per week to remove loose hair and prevent tangles, especially in feathered areas.
  • Ears: Check and clean regularly, particularly after swimming, to help prevent ear infections.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Feed a balanced puppy or large-breed puppy formula and avoid overfeeding to protect growing bones and joints.
  • Activity: Provide multiple short play sessions rather than intense, long runs. Avoid lots of jumping or stairs while they are still growing.
  • Handling: Introduce brushing, ear checks, nail trims, and baths early so grooming becomes a normal, positive experience.
  • Social skills: Expose them gently to new people, animals, and environments to build confidence.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Aim for at least 1‚Äì2 hours of daily physical activity, including walks, off-leash play, and opportunities to swim or retrieve.
  • Coat care: Brushing a few times a week plus occasional trimming of feathering around feet and ears helps keep them tidy.
  • Mind: Training games, scent work, and retrieving tasks keep their active mind engaged and reduce boredom behaviours.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Continue daily walks but shorten or slow them as joints age. Low-impact activities like swimming can be helpful.
  • Comfort: Offer soft beds, help with slippery floors, and make it easy to get in and out of cars or up steps.
  • Health checks: Regular vet exams are important to monitor for joint problems, lumps, or age-related changes.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Exercise in cooler parts of the day, provide shade and fresh water, and watch for signs of overheating.
  • Cold weather: Their dense coat offers some protection, but young, clipped, or thin dogs may benefit from a light coat in very cold conditions.
  • Outdoor adventures: After hikes or swims, check for burrs, foxtails, and ticks, especially in feathered areas and between toes.

Common Health Issues

  • Cancer: Unfortunately, Flat-Coats have a higher risk of certain cancers; report new lumps or changes to your vet promptly.
  • Hip & elbow dysplasia: Maintain a healthy weight, avoid over-exercise in puppies, and follow vet advice on joint health.
  • Ear infections: Their love of water and floppy ears can lead to ear problems‚Äîregular cleaning helps.
  • Bloat (GDV): Like other deep-chested breeds, they may be at risk; discuss prevention and warning signs with your vet.

Training Tips

Flat-Coated Retrievers are bright, friendly, and eager to please, but they can stay puppyish and goofy for a long time. Positive, consistent training and plenty of outlets for their energy help them grow into well-mannered, happy companions.

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start socialisation early with calm, positive introductions to people, dogs, sounds, and places.
  • Teach sit, down, stay, recall, and loose-leash walking using treats, toys, and praise.
  • Practise calm behaviours such as settling on a mat and waiting politely before meals or going through doors.
  • Keep sessions short, fun, and varied‚ÄîFlat-Coats learn best when training feels like a game.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Retrieving games, swimming, and hiking are ideal ways to burn energy and use their natural retrieving instincts.
  • Try dog sports like agility, rally, obedience, or field work for extra mental and physical stimulation.
  • Puzzle feeders, scent games, and ‚Äúfind it‚Äù activities help keep their brain busy on quieter days.
  • Regular off-leash play in safe areas lets them run, explore, and practise recall.

Common Training Challenges

  • Jumping up: Their enthusiasm can lead to jumping. Reward four paws on the floor and ask for a sit before greetings.
  • Pulling on leash: Practise loose-leash skills, rewarding them for walking by your side rather than forging ahead.
  • Easily distracted: Build focus gradually, starting in low-distraction environments and working up to busier places.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Because they love water, supervise swimming and rinse their coat afterwards to remove mud, sand, or pool chemicals.
  • Teach a strong recall and ‚Äúleave it‚Äù cue to help keep them safe around wildlife and other temptations.
  • Use positive training methods; harsh corrections can damage trust and make them anxious or shut down.

Fun Facts

  • Flat-Coated Retrievers were developed in the 1800s in Britain as all-purpose retrievers for both land and water.
  • They share ancestry with other retrievers, including early Labrador-type and Setter-type dogs.
  • They‚Äôre often nicknamed the ‚ÄúPeter Pan of retrievers‚Äù because many Flat-Coats keep a puppy-like outlook well into adulthood.
  • Their glossy, solid-coloured coats are traditionally black, but liver (chocolate) Flat-Coats are also recognised.
  • Known for their cheerful, people-loving nature, many Flat-Coats make wonderful therapy and companion dogs as well as hunting partners.
