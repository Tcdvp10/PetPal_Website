Care

Afghan Hounds are glamorous athletes. Their coat needs serious grooming, and their sighthound body needs regular exercise and safe space to run. With the right routine, they‚Äôre calm, graceful companions at home.

General Care (All Life Stages)

  • Feeding: High-quality diet appropriate for active, medium-to-large dogs. Keep them lean to protect joints.
  • Grooming: Long coat needs thorough brushing several times a week (often daily) plus regular baths to avoid tangles and mats.
  • Ears & feet: Check and clean ears regularly; keep hair between toes tidy to prevent slipping and matting.
  • Environment: Secure fencing is essential‚Äîsighthounds may chase anything that moves.

Puppy Care

  • Growth: Use a balanced large- or medium-breed puppy food; avoid overfeeding to protect growing joints.
  • Grooming training: Teach them early to accept baths, blow-drying, and brushing sessions calmly.
  • Socialisation: Expose them positively to different people, places, and surfaces to prevent shyness.
  • Exercise: Short, gentle play sessions rather than long runs while bones are developing.

Adult Care

  • Exercise: Daily walks plus chances to run in a safely fenced area help them stay fit and content.
  • Coat care: A regular grooming schedule (home care + professional if needed) keeps the coat healthy.
  • Mind: They enjoy free running, scent trails, and sighthound sports like lure coursing.

Senior Care

  • Movement: Lower-impact exercise (shorter walks, gentle play) helps manage arthritis and stiffness.
  • Comfort: Provide soft bedding and draft-free resting spots for their bony frame.
  • Monitoring: Watch for changes in weight, coat quality, or stamina and discuss with your vet.

Seasonal Tips

  • Hot weather: Exercise in cooler parts of the day and provide shade and water; their coat can trap heat.
  • Cold weather: The long coat provides some insulation, but slim dogs may still appreciate a coat in very cold climates.
  • Rain & mud: Long hair can drag dirt‚Äîrinse and dry thoroughly to prevent skin issues and tangles.

Common Health Issues

  • Hip dysplasia: Can cause pain and lameness; maintain a healthy weight and appropriate exercise.
  • Bloat (GDV): Deep-chested dogs are at risk; learn the signs and discuss prevention with your vet.
  • Eye conditions: Cataracts and other eye issues can occur‚Äîregular eye checks are helpful.
  • Allergies & skin issues: Their skin and coat can be sensitive to diet or environment.

Training Tips

Puppy & Basic Manners

  • Start early with sit, down, stay, recall, and loose-leash walking using positive reinforcement.
  • Keep training sessions short and fun; they can get bored with too much repetition.
  • Practise calm handling (ears, feet, brushing) so grooming is easier later.
  • Work on a strong recall, but always remember their natural instinct to chase.

Exercise & Enrichment

  • Daily walks plus opportunities to sprint in a safely fenced area help burn energy.
  • Sighthound sports like lure coursing, or chasing toys, tap into their natural drive.
  • Use puzzle toys and scent games, but expect them to prefer running and exploring over intense obedience drills.
  • Provide safe vantage points‚Äîmany Afghans enjoy quietly watching the world.

Common Training Challenges

  • Recall: Once chasing, they may ignore cues‚Äîso practise in fenced areas and use long lines in open spaces.
  • Sensitivity: They can shut down with harsh corrections; use gentle guidance and high-value rewards instead.
  • Household rules: Be consistent about furniture, jumping up, and door manners to avoid bad habits.
  • Prey drive: Be cautious around small animals‚ÄîAfghans were bred to hunt.

Health & Safety Notes

  • Warm up and cool down properly around intense running to protect muscles and joints.
  • Use a secure harness or well-fitting collar; never let them off-leash near roads or wildlife.
  • Because they‚Äôre slim and athletic, protect them from high-impact collisions and slippery surfaces.

Fun Facts

  • Afghan Hounds were originally bred to hunt in the mountains and deserts of Afghanistan.
  • They‚Äôre powerful sighthounds, built for speed and agility over rough terrain.
  • Their long coat once helped protect them from harsh weather and rough landscapes.
  • Afghan Hounds are considered one of the oldest dog breeds, with a long history in art and legend.
  • Their dramatic looks have made them popular in fashion photography and advertising.
