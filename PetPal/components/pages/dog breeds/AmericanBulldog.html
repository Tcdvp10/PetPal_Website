Care

American Bulldogs are athletic and people-oriented. They need daily exercise, structure, and close contact with their families to stay happy and well-behaved.

General Care

  • Brush once or twice a week to remove loose hair and keep the coat shiny.
  • Clean facial wrinkles (if present) and keep skin folds dry.
  • Provide comfortable bedding to protect joints and elbows.
  • Feed a balanced diet and monitor weight ‚Äì extra kilos are hard on their joints.

Puppy Care

  • Use large-breed puppy food to support proper bone growth.
  • Avoid forced running and jumping while bones and joints are developing.
  • Socialise early with many people, dogs, sounds, and places.
  • Teach gentle handling, mouth manners, and calm behaviour from the beginning.

Adult Care

  • Provide at least an hour of daily exercise: walks, play, training, or dog sports.
  • They enjoy tug, fetch, and strength games when rules and impulse control are taught.
  • Keep up with regular vet care, vaccinations, and parasite prevention.

Senior Care

  • Shorter walks or gentle play sessions to keep muscles toned.

Training Tips

Intelligent, strong, and sometimes stubborn, American Bulldogs need clear rules and positive, confident training. When they know what‚Äôs expected, they are eager to please.

Basic Training

  • Start early with sit, down, stay, recall, and loose-leash walking.
  • Use reward-based methods (treats, toys, praise) to keep them engaged.
  • Focus on impulse control: waiting at doors, not jumping, taking treats gently.
  • Enroll in puppy or basic obedience classes for extra structure.

  • Daily walks plus playtime or training games.

Safety Notes

  • Watch them in hot weather ‚Äì many do not tolerate heat well.
